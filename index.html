Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions homeowners in Frisco have about whole-home surge protection and our installation process.

Whole-home surge protection is installed at your main electrical panel, where power first enters your house. It monitors every circuit and reacts instantly to dangerous voltage spikes, redirecting excess energy safely to ground before it can travel deeper into your home. This protects large appliances (HVAC, fridge, washer/dryer), built-in systems, and outlets throughout the house. Traditional plug-in power strips only guard the specific devices plugged into them, offer limited surge capacity, and cannot protect hard-wired equipment like your AC unit, garage door opener, or built-in smart systems. Whole-home protection works alongside quality point-of-use strips, creating a layered defense for all of your electronics.
For safety, we do temporarily shut off power at your main panel while installing and wiring the surge protection device. For most Frisco homes, the actual installation takes about 60–90 minutes, and total on-site time is typically under three hours, including inspection, labeling, and a walkthrough. We schedule the work at a time that’s convenient for you, clearly communicate when power will be off, and ensure everything is back online and tested before we leave. In many cases, homeowners simply plan around a short downtime window and are pleasantly surprised by how seamless the process feels.
While exact pricing depends on your panel configuration and any unique requirements, most standard Frisco homes fall within a predictable range that is often less than the cost of replacing one major appliance or premium TV. When you factor in the value of your HVAC system, smart home devices, kitchen appliances, entertainment equipment, and office technology, the return on investment becomes clear. Many of our customers see surge protection as a one-time upgrade that continually reduces risk and potential repair costs for the entire lifespan of their home.
In most cases, we can integrate whole-home surge protection directly into your existing panel without major modifications. When we arrive, we evaluate your panel brand, available breaker space, grounding, and overall condition. If we identify any safety issues or outdated components, we will explain them clearly and outline options before proceeding. Many modern Frisco homes are already well-suited for a straightforward surge protection install, but even with older panels, we can usually provide a solution that brings you up to current safety standards while adding protection.
Properly installed whole-home surge protection does not void manufacturer warranties or homeowners insurance policies; in fact, it is often seen as a positive risk reduction measure. Many of the surge devices we recommend include their own limited warranties for connected equipment, and the documentation we provide can support you if you ever need to file an insurance claim related to electrical damage. While every policy is different, we are happy to share typical wording and best practices you can discuss with your agent to make sure you are fully informed.
